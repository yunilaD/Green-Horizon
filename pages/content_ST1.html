Introduction to Green Horizon

Green Horizon focuses on protecting, restoring, and promoting the sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems. This includes sustainably managing forests, combating desertification, halting and reversing land degradation, and stopping biodiversity loss. Our land is home to millions of species and provides food, shelter, and livelihoods for billions of people.

Terrestrial ecosystems provide habitat for countless species and essential services for human well-being

Green Horizon is not just about preserving natural beauty; it's about maintaining the delicate balance of ecosystems that support all life on Earth. Forests, wetlands, mountains, and drylands form interconnected systems that provide clean air, fresh water, climate regulation, and natural resources that sustain our existence.

Key Challenges Facing Life on Land

Despite their importance, terrestrial ecosystems face unprecedented threats from human activities. Deforestation, agricultural expansion, urbanization, pollution, and climate change are driving habitat loss and degradation at alarming rates.

Deforestation

Every year, approximately 10 million hectares of forest are destroyed, primarily for agricultural expansion. This not only eliminates habitat for countless species but also releases stored carbon, contributing to climate change.

Land Degradation

Poor land management practices, including overgrazing, intensive farming, and industrial activities, have degraded about 25% of the Earth's land area. This reduces productivity, threatens food security, and diminishes biodiversity.

Deforestation continues to threaten forest ecosystems worldwide

Desertification

Desertification affects over 3.2 billion people worldwide, particularly in dryland regions. This process turns productive land into desert, driven by climate change and unsustainable land use practices.

Biodiversity Loss: A Crisis Unfolding

We are currently experiencing what scientists call the sixth mass extinction event in Earth's history—and the first one caused by a single species: humans. Unlike previous extinction events, the current crisis is occurring at an unprecedented rate.

Many iconic species face extinction due to habitat loss and other human activities

The loss of biodiversity has profound implications for ecosystem functioning and the services they provide. Each species plays a unique role in its ecosystem, and their disappearance can trigger cascading effects throughout the food web.

Causes of Biodiversity Loss

  • Habitat destruction and fragmentation
  • Overexploitation of natural resources
  • Pollution of air, water, and soil
  • Introduction of invasive species
  • Climate change altering habitats faster than species can adapt

The economic cost of biodiversity loss is estimated to be in the trillions of dollars annually, affecting industries from agriculture to pharmaceuticals, which rely on genetic diversity for innovation and resilience.

Conservation Efforts: Protecting What Remains

Around the world, governments, organizations, and communities are working to protect and restore terrestrial ecosystems. These efforts range from establishing protected areas to implementing sustainable land management practices and restoring degraded landscapes.

Protected Areas

Protected areas, including national parks, wildlife reserves, and indigenous territories, are crucial for conserving biodiversity. Currently, about 15% of the Earth's land is under some form of protection, though the quality and effectiveness of protection vary widely.

Reforestation efforts help restore degraded ecosystems and sequester carbon

Sustainable Land Management

Sustainable land management practices, such as agroforestry, conservation agriculture, and integrated pest management, can help maintain productivity while minimizing environmental impacts. These approaches often draw on indigenous knowledge and modern science to create resilient landscapes.

Restoration

Ecological restoration aims to recover degraded, damaged, or destroyed ecosystems. The UN has declared 2021-2030 the Decade on Ecosystem Restoration, with ambitious goals to restore millions of hectares of degraded land worldwide.

Success Stories: Hope for the Future

Despite the challenges, there are inspiring examples of successful conservation and restoration efforts around the world. These stories demonstrate that with commitment, resources, and innovative approaches, we can reverse the trends of ecosystem degradation and species loss.

The Great Green Wall

The Great Green Wall initiative aims to restore 100 million hectares of degraded land across the Sahel region of Africa by 2030. This ambitious project is not just planting trees but creating a mosaic of sustainable land use systems that benefit both people and nature.

Costa Rica's Forest Recovery

Costa Rica has doubled its forest cover from 26% in 1983 to over 52% today through a combination of protected areas, payments for ecosystem services, and ecotourism. This remarkable recovery shows that deforestation trends can be reversed with the right policies and incentives.

Costa Rica's successful reforestation efforts have doubled the country's forest cover

Wildlife Comeback in Europe

Many wildlife species in Europe, including wolves, bears, and bison, are making a comeback after centuries of decline. This recovery is due to legal protection, habitat restoration, and changing attitudes toward wildlife conservation.

Get Involved: Taking Action for Life on Land

Everyone can contribute to protecting and restoring terrestrial ecosystems. Whether through individual actions, community initiatives, or supporting conservation organizations, your involvement makes a difference.

Plant Native Species

Create habitat in your garden or community by planting native trees, shrubs, and flowers that support local wildlife.

Choose Sustainable Products

Support companies that commit to deforestation-free supply chains and sustainable land management practices.

Volunteer

Join local conservation groups for activities like tree planting, invasive species removal, and habitat restoration.

Advocate

Support policies that protect natural habitats, promote sustainable land use, and fund conservation efforts.

By taking action for Life on Land, you contribute to a future where humans and nature thrive together in harmony. Every effort, no matter how small, adds up to create meaningful change for our planet.
